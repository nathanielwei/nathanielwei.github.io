Zikai (Nathaniel) Wei is a quantitative AI researcher focused on AI-driven quantitative investment and deep learning. He has industry experience at a top-tier hedge fund in Hong Kong and a top-tier asset management firm in mainland China, where he designed, backtested, and deployed deep learning strategies across large- and mid-cap equity indices (including CSI 300, CSI 500, and CSI 1000), combining factor investing, active portfolio construction, and event-driven approaches.

He earned his PhD in Information Engineering in 2023 from The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), under Prof. Xiaoou Tang and Prof. Dahua Lin at the Multimedia Laboratory (MMLab). He previously completed an MPhil in Applied Mathematics at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (2017) and a BA in Finance and Banking at Shenzhen University (2013).

## Research & Industry Focus

His work spans end-to-end deep learning for active investing, adaptive factor modeling, market regime detection, and event-driven prediction. In academia, he was among the first to propose an end-to-end deep learning framework for active investing ([E2EAI](/publication/2023-11-01-e2eai-end-to-end-deep-learning-framework-for-active-investing)), and is co-first author on work introducing end-to-end event-driven investing ([Janus-Q](/publication/2026-02-23-janus-q-end-to-end-event-driven-trading-via-hierarchical-gated-reward-modeling)). His publications appear at venues including IJCAI, ICAIF, NeurIPS and ECCV workshops, *Soft Computing*, and arXiv.

With a multidisciplinary background integrating finance, mathematical modeling, and artificial intelligence, he aims to bridge rigorous academic research and practical quantitative investment.
